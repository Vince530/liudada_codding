Tune ASF

ASF is an adaptive filter that can apply smoothing and sharpening to different parts in images, depending on the detection of edges. It smooths areas with less detail to remove noise and sharpens edges to increase the sharpness of the image.

  1. On the Pipeline tab, click ASF and then click a region in the Regions group.
  2. Click Load Image and select a resolution chart image that was captured under the specified lighting condition.
  3. Click Region Data and expand the Asf 30 Rgn Data navigation tree.
    1. Set layer 1 symmetric kernel parameters based on light conditions.
    2. Tune layer 1 gain strenght based on level and activity to enhance steep edges and textures.
    3. Set layer 2 kernel as the default.
    4. Tune layer 2 gain strength based on level and activity to enhance thick edges and local edge enhancements.
    5. If haloing occurs, tune gain contrast to reduce gain strength around the edges.
    6. If skin appears dirty, tune skin gain to reduce sharpening on skin.
    7. Tune smoothing strength to make edges smoother.
    8. Tune clamp if haloing persists after tuning gain contrast.
  4. Click Simulate.

Kernel

  1. On the Kernel tab, select HPF Symmetric Coeff, LPF Symmetric Coeff, or Activity Band Pass Coeff from the list and adjust the values as needed.

LUT

  1. Click LUT.
    1. On the Gain LUT tab, use the three sliders to adjust Overall Gain, Shadow Boost, and Highlight Boost.
    2. On the Negative Gain tab, select adjust the Negative Gain, Negative Shadow, and Negative Highlight sliders to change gain LUT scaling.
    3. On the Detail tab, adjust the Detail, Noise, and Halo sliders as needed.
      If haloing occurs around chroma edges when contrast is not high, tune gain chroma. If edge alignment is unnatural for textures, tune the thresholds for edge alighnment not to detect textures as edges.
    4. If image corners are noisy or have less details, adjust the Radial Gain and Radial Activity sliders on the Radial tab.
  2. Click Sigma LUT RNR to adjust the RNR control points.
    The radial control circles split the image into four knee points (Radial Anchor[0] though Radial Anchor[3]), where each point has a different noise reduction value. Moving the slider right increases the circle radius and moves the location of the noise.

To confirm the module performs as expected:

  1. Capture a TE42 image.
  2. Verify the following:
    1. Flat area has no dots or artifacts from this module.
    2. Details are visible and look acceptable.
    3. Edges are strong.
    4. Corners are not noisy or less sharpened from the rest of the image.
    5. Halos are not present.
    6. Skin appears smooth.
  3. Repeat process for each region.
  4. Click File > Save to Project to save any changes made.
